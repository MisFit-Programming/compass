Introduction

The 6mx compass identifies personality facets necessary to influence behavior. It’s useful in sales, interrogations, and life-or-death situations. With practice, operators can intuitively read and guide behavior effectively in under six minutes.

Section 1: Values

Values describe the 'why' behind people's behaviors:

  • CONNECTION: Prioritizing relationships with others.
  • INFORMATION: Pursuing knowledge and data.
  • RECOGNITION: Seeking acknowledgment from others.
  • EXPERIENCE: Valuing life experiences.
  • FREEDOM: Valuing liberty and independence.
  • GROWTH: Seeing success as ongoing improvement.

Section 2: Emotional Needs

Emotional needs influence people’s behaviors. Primary needs include:

  • SIGNIFICANCE: Desire to feel important and acknowledged.
  • APPROVAL/RECOGNITION: Seeking approval from others.
  • ACCEPTANCE: Needing to belong to a group.

Secondary needs include:

  • INTELLIGENCE: Wanting to be seen as smart.
  • PITY: Seeking sympathy for hardships.
  • STRENGTH/POWER: Seeking to appear strong and powerful.

Section 3: The Decision Map

The Decision Map explains decision-making strategies:

  • DEVIANCE: Standing out and breaking social norms.
  • NOVELTY: Being an early adopter.
  • SOCIAL: Making decisions to connect with others.
  • CONFORMITY: Aligning with peer group behaviors.
  • INVESTMENT: Seeking valuable returns.
  • NECESSITY: Focusing on essential actions.

Section 4: Representational Systems

People process information using three main representational systems:

  • VISUAL: Thinking in pictures.
  • AUDITORY: Thinking in sounds or words.
  • KINESTHETIC: Thinking in feelings or actions.

Section 5: Pronouns

Pronouns indicate a person’s social focus:

  • Self Pronouns: Focus on “I” or “me.”
  • Team Pronouns: Focus on “we” or “our.”
  • Other Pronouns: Focus on external groups, “they.”

Section 6: Adjectives

Adjectives reveal internal processes. Positive adjectives help build rapport, while negative adjectives indicate behaviors to avoid for better communication.

Section 7: Gestural Hemispheric Tendency

Gestural Hemispheric Tendency (GHT) identifies which side a person gestures to when discussing positive or negative topics. Knowing this helps guide communication.

Section 8: Locus of Control

Locus of Control reflects whether someone feels in control of their life:

  • Internal: Belief that they control their life.
  • External: Belief that external factors control their life.

Section 9: Handedness

Handedness (right or left) reflects brain hemisphere dominance and can indicate a person's decision-making style.

Section 10: Affective Mapping

Affective Mapping identifies emotional responses in behaviors such as lip compression, eyebrow flashes, or breathing patterns, which indicate withheld opinions or uncertainty.
