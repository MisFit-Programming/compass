Using the Compass

The 6mx compass identifies the personality facets needed to create behavioral results. It can be used in sales, interrogations, and life-or-death situations. The goal is to make this intuitive, allowing operators to create results through observation and response.

A skilled practitioner can gather all the information in minutes—no more than six minutes should be required.

Section 1: Values

On the 6mx compass, values describe the 'why' behind behaviors, motivating actions and decisions:

  • CONNECTION: The most important thing is connections with others.
  • INFORMATION: Data and knowledge are the keys to success.
  • RECOGNITION: Success is being acknowledged by others.
  • EXPERIENCE: Life is based on experiences.
  • FREEDOM: Liberty and independence define success.
  • GROWTH: Success is an ongoing process of growth.

Section 2: Emotional Needs

Emotional needs shape people's behaviors. Understanding these needs lets you influence them effectively.

Primary Needs

  • SIGNIFICANCE: Desire to feel important and acknowledged by others.
  • APPROVAL/RECOGNITION: Seeking approval and compliments.
  • ACCEPTANCE: Focus on belonging to a group.

Secondary Needs

  • INTELLIGENCE: Displaying intellectual prowess, avoiding being seen as uneducated.
  • PITY: Seeking sympathy for hardships, wanting acknowledgment for struggles.
  • STRENGTH/POWER: Displaying dominance and seeking validation through power.

Section 3: The Decision Map

The 6mx compass maps decision-making strategies, allowing you to tailor how you present options:

  • DEVIANCE: Decisions are made to stand out or break norms.
  • NOVELTY: Prioritizing cutting-edge, new experiences.
  • SOCIAL: Decisions are made to connect with others.
  • CONFORMITY: Aligning behaviors with peer expectations.
  • INVESTMENT: Focusing on valuable returns from actions.
  • NECESSITY: Making decisions based on practical needs.

Section 4: Representational Systems

People process information in different ways, which can be categorized as representational systems:

  • VISUAL: Thinking in images and pictures.
  • AUDITORY: Processing information through sounds and words.
  • KINESTHETIC: Thinking through feelings and actions.

Section 5: Pronouns

Pronouns reflect a person's social focus. Pay attention to how they use "I," "we," or "they" to understand their perspective.

  • Self Pronouns: Focus on "I" or "me".
  • Team Pronouns: Focus on "we" or "us".
  • Other Pronouns: Focus on external groups ("they").

Section 6: Adjectives

Adjectives reveal internal processes, and by identifying the adjectives someone uses, you can better understand their thought patterns.

Section 7: Gestural Hemispheric Tendency

GHT identifies which side a person gestures to when discussing positive or negative topics, helping you guide communication effectively.

Section 8: Locus of Control

Locus of Control reflects whether someone believes they control their life (internal) or if external factors have more influence (external).

Section 9: Handedness

Handedness reflects brain hemisphere dominance and can indicate how a person makes decisions.

Section 10: Affective Mapping

Affective Mapping identifies emotional responses through behaviors like eyebrow flashes, lip compression, and breathing patterns.
